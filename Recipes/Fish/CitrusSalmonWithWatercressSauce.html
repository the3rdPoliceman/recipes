Citrus Salmon with Watercress Sauce
SALMON
600g Skinless Salmon Fillet
Zest of 1 orange
Zest of 1 lemon
3 Tablespoons Salt
2 Tablespoons Sugar
1 Tablespoon Ground White Pepper
200ml Olive Oil
ORANGES
2 Oranges
100ml Water
80g Sugar
1/2 Teaspoon White Wine Vinegar
WATERCRESS SAUCE
150g Watercress (Rocket also works)
25g Butter
25g Chives, Finely Chopped
25g Lumpfish Roe Caviar, if you’re feeling rich!
    Preparing the salmon
  1. Trim the edges of the salmon so that the whole piece is roughly the same thickness
  2. Combine the orange and lemon zest, sugar, salt and pepper in a bowl and mix well
  3. Completely cover the fish in the marinade and wrap it tightly in kitchen foil
  4. Put on a plate in the refrigerator with a second plate on top to squash it a little, and leave to marinate for 3 hours
  5. Remove the fish from the foil, rinse off the marinade and dry it with kitchen paper
  6. Cut into individual portions and return to the refrigerator
    7. Preparing the oranges
  7. Cut the oranges into segments, and place in a bowl
  8. Boil the sugar and water in a saucepan to dissolve the sugar, then add the vinegar
  9. Pour the mixture over the oranges and leave to cool
    10. Preparing the watercress sauce
  10. Boil the watercress in salted water until tender (about 5 minutes), then drain and steep it in cold water to stop it cooking
  11. Place the watercress in a blender and blend to create a sauce - you may need to add a little water to help it blend
  12. Pass the sauce through a sieve if it seems a bit stringy
    13. To complete
  13. Place the pieces of salmon in one or more zip-lock bags along with the olive oil, and squeeze all the air out of the zip lock bags and seal
  14. Sous vide at 43°C for 15 minutes (cooking longer won’t do any harm)
  15. Remove the salmon, take it out of the bags and drain on kitchen paper. It should still look raw but it will be cooked
  16. Meanwhile, bring the watercress sauce to a simmer, whisk in the butter and add salt to taste
  17. Warm the orange sections in the syrup and then drain them on kitchen paper
  18. Spread the chives on a dish and press the tops of the salmon pieces in the chives to coat them
    19. To serve
  19. Place orange sections in the center of each plate, spoon the sauce around the outside, and set the fish on top of the oranges
  20. Put a little fish roe on top
