French Apple Cake
cm round cake
120g butter
0.25 teaspoon ground allspice
1.5kg apples (sour or a mix)
140g sugar
2.5g salt
30ml rum or brandy
80g flour
5g baking powder
2 eggs
1 vanilla pod
  1. Heat the oven to 180C. Grease the cake pan with butter, then dust with flour and tip out the excess
  2. Heat 100g of the butter in a pan until it melts and the milk solids turn golden brown. Pour into a bowl along with the allspice
  3. Peel and core the apples and slice each apple into about 12 wedges
  4. Add the apples to a large frying pan along with 20g butter, 30g sugar and the salt. Fry over medium heat until the apples have released most of their moisture and started to brown
  5. Add the rum or brandy and cook until evaporated, then remove the apples from the pan and allow to cool completely
  6. In a large bowl, whisk the eggs, vanilla and 100g sugar until light and fluffy, then gradually whisk in the browned butter
  7. Slowly beat in the flour and baking powder until just combined, then fold in the apples and transfer to the cake pan
  8. Sprinkle the remaining 10g of sugar over the top, and bake until well browned (35 to 40 minutes)
  9. Let cool in the pan, then run a knife around the inside of the pan before slicing
Notes
  • Can me made ahead and then warmed up for serving in a low oven. 100C, covered, for 20 mins works
  • Might work well with rhubarb too
