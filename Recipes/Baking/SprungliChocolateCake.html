Sprungli Chocolate Cake
xcm bread pan
88g egg white (about 4)
100g sugar (1)
140g egg yolk (about 6)
40g sugar (2)
32g white flour
22g cocoa powder
40g ground hazelnuts
2g salt
30g butter
400g chocolate
400g full cream
1.5g salt
  1. Sponge
  2. Melt the butter in a pan and let it cool
  3. Wash a bowl and whisk really well, wiping with some vinegar on kitchen paper to remove any possible grease. Whisk the egg white together with sugar (1) to stiff peaks
  4. Beat sugar (2) and yolk for about 10 minutes
  5. Mix white flour, cocoa powder, and nuts together
  6. Sieve the dry ingredients onto the egg yolk mixture and fold in carefully, followed by the egg whites and finally the liquid butter
  7. Butter the bread form, pour in the batter, and bake at 170C for 40 minutes
  8. Remove from form, cool on a rack. If you want to leave it overnight, cover it with foil, but make sure you let it cool first
  9. Ganache
  10. Warm the cream until it is boiling. Take the pan off the heat, add in the chopped chocolate and salt, and mix until ganache forms
  11. Mix with immersion blender to emulsify well
  12. Place in a shallow bowl, covered with food film, at room temperature. Leave it alone
  13. Assembling
  14. Trim 1.5mm from each side and end of the cake
  15. Discard the top crust, and split remaining cake into 3 layers
  16. Put ganache into piping bag
  17. Reassemble in the tin it was baked in, first lining it with cling film or acetate so it can be easily removed
  18. Spread a thin layer of ganache on the side/ends and a fairly thin layer on the base
  19. Then take a layer of cake, apply ganache to the edges generously and add to the tin
  20. Repeat until done
  21. Then spread all remaining ganache on top, level off
  22. Allow to cool and harden, remove from form
  23. Fix any imperfections with some heated knife
  24. Dust the top with cocoa
