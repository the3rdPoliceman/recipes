Overnight Bread
400g white flour
100g wholemeal flour
1 teaspoon dried yeast
2 teaspoons fine salt
    PRE-FERMENT
  1. On the night before, mix 200g white flour, 200g water and 0.25 teaspoons yeast. Leave sitting out until the next day
  2. Mix in the remaining ingredients
    3. FOLDING
  3. oil your hands so the dough doesn't stick to you
  4. grab one side of the dough, stretch it a bit and fold it onto the other (like you're closing a stretchy book), then turn the bowl 90 degrees
  5. repeat 15-20 times
  6. Leave until it doubles in size (maybe 90 mins)
    7. SHAPING
  7. Flour a work surface and flip the dough out onto it. Then shape it - this video from the 2:00 minutes shows how (The idea is to get some tension into the surface of the loaf)
  8. Cover a tea towel in flour, put the dough on the tea towel, and put the whole thing in a bowl. Like this
  9. Leave it for an hour
    10. BAKING
  10. Heat the over to 230C
  11. put baking paper or flour onto a baking tray
  12. flip the dough onto the tray
  13. slash a cross into the top of it, like this
  14. If you have a big metal bowl, cover the dough with that - it'll help it to puff up more
  15. Put it in the oven
  16. After 20 minutes, turn the heat down to 200C, and take the metal bowl off
  17. Cook until it's pretty dark brown
