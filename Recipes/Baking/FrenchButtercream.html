French Buttercream
25g water
70g fine sugar
1 egg yolk
1 egg
200g butter, at room temp
  1. Bring the still mineral water and sugar to a boil. Cook the syrup until it reaches 120C
  2. In the bowl of a mixer fitted with a wire whisk, beat the egg yolks and the eggs until they just become pale
  3. Gradually pour the syrup into the eggs and continue to beat until the mixture has cooled completely (a waterbath helps cool it quicker)
  4. Beat the butter in the bowl of a stand mixer until it is creamy
  5. Gradually incorporate the butter into the egg mixture while beating. If it looks like it's separating, just keep beating
Notes
  • In general, there will be a lot of beating
Variations
  • Vanilla buttercream - beat in 5 to 10 milliliters of vanilla extract at the end (it will look curdled initially)
  • Coffee buttercream - dissolve 2 teaspoons of instant coffee in 15ml of vanilla rum and beat into the buttercream, at the end (it will look curdled initially)
  • Beating in 20g powdered sugar at the end will give you a slightly sweeter, stiffer, less fatty feeling buttercream
