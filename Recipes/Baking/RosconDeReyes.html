Roscón De Reyes
Pre-ferment
100g flour
60ml milk, warm
2g fresh yeast
Flavored Milk
60ml milk
1 cinnamon stick
zest of 1 orange
Dough
330g flour
2 eggs
30ml honey
15g fresh yeast
15ml rum (I used rum vanilla essence)
10ml agua de azar
zest of 0.5 lemons
5g salt
80g sugar
110g butter, cold from the fridge
Decoration
1 egg, beaten
candied orange slices
flaked almonds
To Finish
syrup from the candied oranges
500ml crème pâtissière
1 bean
1 small king
    The Night Before
  1. Make the candied oranges, saving the syrup
  2. Mix the ingredients for the pre-ferment together, leave to sit for 30 minutes, then wrap in cling film and refrigerate overnight
  3. For the flavored milk, heat the milk together with the orange zest and cinnamon stick until nearly boiling, then leave to steep overnight
    4. Making the Dough
  4. Put all the dough ingredients apart from the sugar and butter in a bowl, along with the pre-ferment and flavored milk. Knead for 5 minutes
  5. Add half the sugar and knead a few minutes more. Do the same again with the remaining half of the sugar
  6. Add the butter, and knead for about 10 minutes until completely incorporated. It will become extremely sticky during the kneading, and feel almost like choux dough
  7. Leave to rise for 2 hours, knock back, form into a ball. After 15 minutes, form it into a donut shape for the roscón, leaving about 12cm of a hole in the middle
  8. Leave to rise for 3 hours more, then decorate with the beaten egg, oranges, and almonds
    9. Baking and Filling
  9. Bake for around 20 minutes at 180C with fan
  10. While the roscón is cooking, make the crème pâtissière
  11. When the roscón is done, take it out and leave to cool on a rack
  12. Once cold, split it in half. Then fill it with the cream, and hide the bean and the king
  13. Brush the top with a little of the syrup from the candied oranges
