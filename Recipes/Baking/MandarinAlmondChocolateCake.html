Mandarin, Almond & Chocolate Cake
cm cake
105g unsalted butter, softened
60g fine brown sugar
60g dark brown sugar
2 eggs, separated
1 tsp vanilla extract
60g 90% chocolate, frozen
120g ground almonds
0.33 teaspoon salt
10g cocoa powder
zest of 3 mandarins (if skins are very thin, the entire peel can be dried for a day and blitzed)
0.75tsp baking powder
  1. Heat oven to 150C with fan. Grease and paper your pan
  2. Beat the butter and sugar together until light
  3. Beat in the vanilla and egg yolks, one at a time
  4. Blitz the frozen chocolate until it has the texture of coarse sand
  5. Mix the almonds, chocolate, salt, cocoa, zest and baking powder together
  6. Beat the egg whites until stiff, then combine the butter mix, almond mix and egg wites in a bowl, and beat slowly together until just combined
  7. Bake until skewer comes out clean. It'll probably need an hour, but check after around 50 minutes
Notes
  • Try toasting the almonds first
  • Try a version of this without chocolate and with tea used to infuse the butter
Adapted from River Cafe Capri Torte
