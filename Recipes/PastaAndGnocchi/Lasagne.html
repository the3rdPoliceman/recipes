Lasagne
750g Ragu Bolognese (Roughly 1/4 recipe)
spinach pasta, made with 300g flour, 150g egg, 70g spinach
Bechamel, made with 1.3l milk and 90g flour
60g grated parmesan
    Preparation
  1. Preheat the oven to 160C
  2. Make the pasta and roll into sheets - probably on the thinnest setting, but if that's incredibly thin use the next one up
  3. Cook the pasta sheets for a couple of minutes in salted water, then drain
    4. Assembling
  4. If the ragu was cold, heat it up and stir in enough water that its easily spreadable
  5. Set aside 200g of bechamel to use for covering the top at the end
  6. Spread a very small amount of the ragu in the base of the dish you're using
  7. Add layers of pasta, ragu, bechamel, and parmesan until almost everything is used up. You don't want too much filling between each pasta layer. Ideally, you should end up with at least 6 layers of pasta - more thin layers helps the structure
  8. Top with the bechamel you set aside at the start, a spot of ragu here and there, and a little parmesan
  9. Bake until the top is browning, about 40 minutes. It's better to cook reasonably long at a slow temperature, so the pasta has time to absorb the ragu and bechamel
Notes
  • Calcuate pasta thickness in GSM, like paper. I used the thinnest setting on the pasta roller
