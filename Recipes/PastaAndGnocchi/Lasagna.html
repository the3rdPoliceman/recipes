Lasagna
Half a recipe ragu bolognese
fresh spinach pasta, made with 300g flour
60g grated parmesan
BECHAMEL
1l milk
1 onion
3 bay leaves
4 cloves
90g butter
80g flour
1/3 nutmeg
salt and pepper
    Preparation
  1. Preheat the oven to 170C
  2. Make the pasta and roll into sheets - probably on the thinnest setting, but if that's incredibly thin use the next one up
  3. To make the bechamel, heat the milk with the onion, bay leaves and cloves. Then leave to infuse for around half an hour. Melt the butter, before adding the flour and frying gently for a couple of minutes. Strain the milk and add it to the flour while whisking. Bring it up to the boil and cook it for 5 minutes. Add the nutmeg, and salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Cook the pasta sheets for a couple of minutes in salted water, then drain
    5. Assembling the Lasagna
  5. Spread a very small amount of the ragu in the base of the dish you're using
  6. Add layers of pasta, ragu, bechamel, and parmesan until almost everything is used up. You don't want too much stuff between each pasta layer. You want at least 6 layers ideally - having more thin layers helps the structure of the lasagna
  7. Top with bechamel, a little ragu here and there and parmesan
  8. Bake until the top is browning (about 30 minutes)
