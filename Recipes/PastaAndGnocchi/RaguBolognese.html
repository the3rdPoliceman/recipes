Ragu Bolognese
16 sheets gelatin
1 large onion, finely minced
2 carrots, finely minced
4 ribs celery, finely minced
150g pancetta, finely minced
20g fresh sage leaves, shredded
50g fresh parsley leaves, chopped
60g unsalted butter
2 medium cloves garlic, minced
60ml extra-virgin olive oil
700g ground beef siedfleisch
750g ground pork shoulder
150g chicken livers
500ml dry white or red wine
1L chicken stock
800g can peeled whole tomatoes
250ml milk
2 bay leaves
half a nutmeg
Salt and black pepper
250ml heavy cream
85g finely grated Parmesan cheese
  1. Preheat the oven to 150C
  2. Soak the gelatin sheets in cold water
  3. Mince the vegetables and pancetta in a fine meat grinder, or grate or finely dice. Fry in a wide pan with the butter, sage and half the parsley. You want to fry rather than steam here, and they can start to color very slightly. Add in the garlic towards the end and fry briefly, not coloring.
  4. While the vegetables are frying, fry the ground meats in olive oil, in batches until browned. Rinse the pan out between each batch with a little of the wine and reserve both the wine and the meat.
  5. Blend the chicken livers, and add these and the ground meat to the vegetables, along with all the wine
  6. Reduce until more or less no wine remains
  7. Add the chicken stock, tomatoes, milk, drained gelatin sheets, bay leaves and nutmeg. Season with a little salt and pepper (careful not to add too much as you'll add parmesan later and reduce the ragu)
  8. Bring to a simmer, then cook in the oven for 3-4 hours, until well reduced and the fat has formed a separate layer on top of the meat. Stir every 30 minutes or so.
  9. Stir in the cream, parmesan, and remaining parsley
  10. Season with salt and pepper
