Basque Cheesecake
cm round cheesecake
500g cream cheese, room temp
250ml double cream
180g caster sugar
2.5g salt (optional)
3.5 eggs, beaten
7g flour
  1. Dissolve the cornflour in about 100ml of the double cream - it's easier to start with a little and form a paste, and then gradually add the cream
  2. Put everything in a bowl (preferably a taller narrower one). Fully submerge a stick bleander in there and blend until smooth
  3. Line an 18cm pan with baking paper - one piece for the base and one for the sides. The piece used for the side should extend well above the sides of the pan as the mixture may double in size during cooking
  4. Cook on middle shelf at 220C fan, until the lowest temperature in the cheesecake is 45C. Ideally, it'll still look very liquid, but the top should be chestnut brown
Notes
  • Overcooking it will tend to give a more cakelike texture and it'll seep a little bit as it cools. Getting the temperature right will give a creamier result with less leakage
  • Bubbles on the top will burn too quickly, so aim to get no air bubbles when mixing
  • Next time, reduce sugar by 20%, try taking out at 42C
  • What if we cooked it in a really low oven until it hits our desired final temp to give a more consistent creamy texture throughout, then let it cool before blasting under the grill to color the top?
