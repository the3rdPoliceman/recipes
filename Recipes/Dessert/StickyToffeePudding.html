Sticky Toffee Pudding
by cm cake pan
Pudding
100g dates, roughly chopped
1/2 tsp bicarbonate of soda
150ml boiling water
25g unsalted butter, softened
40g raw brown sugar
40g light brown sugar
1 egg
15ml vanilla rum
90g flour
1g salt
1/2 tsp baking powder
Pinch of ground cloves
Sauce
85g unsalted butter
45g raw brown sugar
45g light brown sugar
105ml double cream
1.5g salt
    Prepare the dates
  1. Add the dates and baking soda to the boiling water. Bring back to the boil, then leave to sit until the dates soften a bit
    2. Make the Sauce
  2. While the dates boil and soften, make the sauce. Add all the sauce ingredients to a pan and bring to the boil, then allow to bubble for a few minutes until it thickens
  3. Butter the cake pan, and pour a third of the sauce in the bottom. Place in the freezer until you're ready to bake the pudding
    4. Make the Pudding
  4. Cream the butter and sugar together until light and fluffy, then add the egg and vanilla and beat well
  5. Blend the softened dates to a puree, and stir into the mix
  6. Mix the flour, salt, baking powder and cloves together, and fold into the mix
  7. Take the pan out of the freezer, and pour the pudding batter in
  8. Bake at 170C until just firm to the touch - about 30 minutes
  9. Poke holes in the pudding with a skewer. Reheat another third of the sauce from earlier and pour over the pudding
  10. Place under a hot grill for a few minutes until it turns a deep brown, but be careful as it can start to burn quite quickly
  11. Warm the final third of the sauce and serve alongside the pudding
Variations
  • Try using hot black tea instead of hot water
