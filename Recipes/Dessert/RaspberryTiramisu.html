Raspberry Tiramisu
500g frozen raspberries
3 eggs
90g sugar
2tsp vanilla extract
375g mascarpone
20g Simple Syrup (50:50 water sugar by weight)
12-16 savoiardi biscuits (enough to make layers)
  1. Leave the raspberries overnight in a sieve over a bowl, to thaw and collect the juice
  2. Have the eggs and mascarpone in the fridge overnight, and take them out 20 minutes before starting to make the tiramisu
  3. In a bowl, whisk the egg whites until almost stiff. Add 20g sugar, and continue whisking until stiff
  4. In a second bowl, whisk the egg yolks with the remaining sugar intil thick and pale. Whisk in the vanilla
  5. In a third bowl, whisk the mascarpone on the slowest speed to soften it up a little. This will help it combine more easily with the other ingredients
  6. Add the egg yolk mix into the mascarpone, and whisk at the slowest setting until smoothly combined
  7. Fold in the egg whites
  8. Mix the juice from the raspberries with the simple syrup. Do this to taste, it should still be quite tart
  9. Dunk the biscuits in the juice until they soak a decent amount of it. The raspberry juice soaks in a lot slower than coffee
  10. Layer up mascarpone, then biscuits, then half the raspberries. Repeat, and finally top with the rest of the mascarpone
  11. Chill for a few hours
Notes
  • The mascarpone tends to want to curdle if overwhipped or mixed with ingredients at different temperatures. That's why care with the temperature and whisking is emphasized here
  • Maybe 2 egg yolks would be enough. 2 whites might also be fine
Serving Suggestions
  • Sprinkle with lime leaf powder, ground up freeze dried raspberries, or cocoa powder
