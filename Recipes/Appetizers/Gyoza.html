Gyoza
gyoza
150g cabbage, finely chopped
1 teaspoon sea salt
250g pork mince
1 cloves garlic, grated
50g garlic chives, finely minced scallions
10g ginger, finely grated
60 ml chicken stock dashi
7ml soy sauce
7ml sake
5ml sesame oil
black pepper
30 gyoza wrappers
15ml sesame oil
  1. Rub the salt into the cabbage and leave it to draw water out, then squeeze out the water
  2. Mix everything up to the pepper together very well, using only enough chicken stock to get it to mix nicely
  3. Fry little bits to check for salt, and correct if necessary
  4. Make the gyoza using water to seal. See here
  5. Heat a pan medium high then add the gyoza one at a time, dipping the base of each dumpling in oil as you add it. Fry without moving until the bottom of the dumplings is quite dark brown
  6. Add a few millimetres of water to the pan, immediately cover with a lid and steam
  7. The idea is that they should be cooked at the same point as th pan is dried
  8. Serve drizzled with a little sesame oil, some soy, or other dipping sauce
