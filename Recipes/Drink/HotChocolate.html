Hot Chocolate
150ml milk
50ml cream
0.1g salt
8g raw brown sugar
35g dark chocolate (naturaplan honduras 70% is current favourite)
  1. Heat the milk, cream, sugar and salt to 80C (steaming, but not quite boiling)
  2. Chop the chocolate, and put in a tall tumbler
  3. Add the milk to the chocolate, and use a stick blender with the chopping blade to blend until the chocolate is fully incorporated. Do this with the head of the blender fully submerged
  4. Now we want to create a slightly thicker feeling texture by adding microbubbles, like in a latte. To do this, blend a little a with the blender at the surface to introduce some air, then submerge and blend again thoroughly in order to disperse the air in evenly sized, very small bubbles
Notes
  • The amount of sugar needed will vary with the chocolate.
    • 7-8g is good for Lindt 70%, and Naturaplan Ecuador 70% and Honduras 70%
    • Naturaplan Madagascar 82% needs about 12g
    Variations
    • A teaspoon of vanilla essence can be added to the milk
    • 10 -15ml of rum can be added after the milk has been heated
    • Create a flavoured milk, by warming it along with a couple of cardamom pods, a tonka bean, or some thin shavings of orange zest. Leave to steep for 20 minutes before straining out
