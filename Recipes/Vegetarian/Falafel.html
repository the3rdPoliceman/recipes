Egyptian Falafel (Ta'ameya)
Batter
200g Split Fava beans
50g Spring Onions
50g Leeks
30g Coriander
30g Parsley
2 Cloves of garlic
1 Tsp Dried Coriander
3/4 Tsp Cumin
1/2 Tsp Black Pepper
1/4 Tsp Cayenne
10g salt
300ml water
Topping
1 tbsp Coriander seeds
1 tbsp Sesame seeds
Frying
1l vegetable oil
  1. Soak the beans overnight, and then drain
  2. Roughly chop the greens and herbs, and then combine all the batter ingredients in a large bowl
  3. Chop everything very finely in a blender. Transfer the mix to a sieve, and leave until 200ml of the water has drained out (The water could be drained faster by squeezing in cheesecloth)
  4. Beat thoroghly with an electric whisk until the batter aquires a mousse like consistency
  5. Heat the oil to 180C
  6. Form the falafel either by spooning straight into the oil or using the method here. The video method is actually much easier than it looks
Notes
  • The main reason for the water in this recipe is that it makes the blending much easier. Maybe with a food processor, it wouldn't be necessary
  • How you form the balls will have a big effect on the texture, so avoid squeezing them into balls as they'll be too dense
