Tortilla de Patatas
1350g Potatoes
150g onion
25g Salt
10 eggs
500ml olive Oil
A good non stick pan is essential
  1. Peel the potatoes and use a knife to lever off chunks of the potato rather than chop them
  2. Chop the onion finely and put in a bowl with the potatoes and salt
  3. Leave for 20 minutes so water comes out of the potatoes, then drain (maybe dry the potatoes and onion also?)
  4. Heat the olive oil until it bubbles quite quickly when a piece of potato is put in, but doesn't go crazy
  5. Add the potatoes and onion and cook until the potatoes are tender and the onion has softened
  6. When the potatoes are almost completely cooked through, turn up the heat and continue cooking until the edges of the potatoes become golden
  7. Crack three or four eggs into a bowl, mix the drained potatoes into it (a bit of oil clinging to the potatoes does no harm). Stir together well
  8. Add the rest of the eggs and stir some more
  9. To cook the final tortilla, get a pan very hot and add a few tablespoons of the cooking oil from before
  10. Add the mix, and flip or mix for the first minute or two until curds start forming. Then allow it to settle in the pan for a minute or two to brown the bottom
  11. Drip some oil over the top of the omlette and flip onto a plate or lid. Slide back into the pan and cook the other side briefly until the middle is semi solid
  12. Slide out onto a plate to serve
