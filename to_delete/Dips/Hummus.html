Hummus
2 cans chickpeas
1 teaspoon baking soda
90ml lemon juice
2 cloves garlic
1 teaspoon salt
240g tahini
60ml ice cold water
Â½ teaspoon ground cumin
45ml olive oil
  1. Drain and rinse the chickpeas, then put in a saucepan with the baking soda and enough water to cover the chickpeas by 5cm
  2. Boil them for 20 minutes
  3. While they boil, put the lemon juice, garlic and salt in a blender and blend quickly. Leave for 10 minutes so the garlic flavor mellows
  4. Add the tahini and ice water and blend a bit more
  5. Once the chickpeas are done, drain them and put them in lots cold water to cool down
  6. The hummus is better if you get rid of the skins - it's not important to get them all, but it's easy to get most of them. To do this, mix the chickpeas around roughly in the water to detach the skins. The skins float and the chickpeas sink, so you can pour away the water and skins at the same time
  7. Add the chickpeas, cumin and olive oil to the blender, and blend again until really smooth
  8. Add more salt and lemon if needed
