Making Sushi
DETAILED INFO
La Petit Noisette provides a lot of very good detailed information about sushi making from a guy who worked in a 3 star michelin sushi restaurant in japan. He includes very specific brands, recipes, techniques etc.
GENERAL NOTES
Shiny side goes out when making Maki
Sesame seeds should be toasted
Curing salmon with salt for an hour, before rinsing in vinegar will firm it up - Teruya Noriyoshi, Nobu

SHAPING NIGIRI
The aim is to squeeze tight enough that it doesn't fall apart, but not tighter as it will become chewier
  • Rinse your hands with vinegar water (one hand then rub them together well)
  • Topping on smallest finger joints
  • Grab rice ball
  • Grab some wasabi on fingertip
  • Smear wasabli on the topping
  • Place the rice on the topping
  • Thumb push it down, into a squashed beanbag shape
  • Roll over
  • Squeeze sides in
  • Squeeze down with 1 or 2 fingers
  • Rotate 180
  • Side squeeze
  • Squeeze down
  • Repeat squeezing if needed
  • Brush with soy or nikiri

FRIED NIGIRI WITH SPICY TUNA
40g Tuna
5g Sriracha
Small amount of chives or spring onion

Form the sushi rice into a flattish brick 2cm high, 4cm wide, and long enough to make however many nigiri you want - you'll need 2cm per nigiri
Refrigerate the log until firm (preferably overnight)
Cut the brick into individual nigiri portions, deep fry in neutral oil, and top with the spicy tuna

CURING FISH
SALT CURED TUNA
Piece of tuna, around 1 inch thick
Fleur de Sel, or other similarly textured salt
Coat the piece of tuna thoroghly with Fleur de Sel. Leave on a grid for 30 minutes in the fridge, then rinse off the salt and dry

CITRUS CURED SALMON
200g piece of salmon, around 1 inch thick
1 tbsp Fleur de Sel, or other similarly textured salt
2 tsp white sugar
1 tsp white pepper
Zest of 1/2 orange
Zest of 1/2 lemon
Mix everything except the salmon together. Coat the salmon with the cure and leave on a grid in the fridge for 30 minutes, the rinse off the cure and dry

TAMAGO OMELETTE
2 eggs
1 egg yolk
1/2 tsp mirin
pinch of salt
Whisk everything together, fry a thin layer and roll up. Then fry another layer, and roll the existing omlette up in the new layer. Repeat until you've used all the egg. Take out and use the bamboo maki maker to form it into the final shape.
