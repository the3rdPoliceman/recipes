Brining
BASIC BRINE RECIPE
The basic ingredients for brining are
  • water
  • 5% salt
  • 3% sugar

These are heated together to dissolve the salt and sugar. The brine is then thoroughly chilled, before the meat is placed in it for a specific amount of time. To reduce the chilling time, you can heat only a portion of the water along with the salt and sugar, and then add the rest of the water at the end to speed up the cooling process

After brining, the meat should be allowed to rest for a while to allow the salt to disperse evenly through the meat(a few hours for a chicken, up to a day for a large turkey)

BRINING TIMES
  • Small things e.g. chicken breasts, pork chops - 2 hours
  • Big things - 4-6hrs per kg
ADDING FLAVOR
Common optional flavorings
Almost anything that pairs well with your meat can be added to the brine. Very common additions include
  • Onion
  • Carrot
  • Garlic
  • Celery
  • Peppercorns
  • Parsley
  • Thyme
  • Bay Leaves
Optional flavorings for pork
Honey or brown sugar are a great alternative to white sugar. Other common additions are
  • Sage
  • Garlic
  • Juniper
  • Bay Leaf
Optional flavorings for chicken
Any of the common optional ingredients above are good, along with
  • Tarragon
  • Lemon
Optional flavorings for fish
  • Garlic
  • Dill
  • Lemon
