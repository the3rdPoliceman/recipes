Chocolate
TEMPERING
Basic Concept
Chocolate forms the highest proportion of stable i.e. snappy well tempered, crystals when held above the melting point of the unstable crystals and below the melting point of the stable crystals

Method
Tempering chocolate consists of three stages:
  • Heating the chocolate well above the temperature necessary to melt all the crystals (Peak Temperature)
  • The temperature of the chocolate is then allowed to fall to a specific temperature (Cooling Temperature) to form a new set of starter crystals
  • Finally, the chocolate is heated again, while agitating frequently, to a precise final temperature which ensures the unstable crystals are all melted and only the desirable crystals remain. It can be held indefinitely at this temperature
Example temperatures from On Food and Cooking, as a rough guide
Chocolate Peak Temperature Cooling Temperature Final Temperature
Dark 45–50C 28–29C 31–32C
Milk 40–45C 27–28C 30–31C
White 40C 24–25C 27–28C
