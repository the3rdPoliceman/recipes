Chocolate
TEMPERING
Basic concept: Chocolate forms the highest proportion of stable i.e. snappy well tempered, crystals when held above the melting point of the unstable crystals and below the melting point of the stable crystals

Sous Vide Tempering: The method consists of heating the chocolate well above the temperature necessary to melt all the crystals. Then the temperature is dropped to form a new set of starter crystals, before heating again to a precise temperature while agitating frequently to melt the unstable crystals and leave only the crystals you want remaining.
