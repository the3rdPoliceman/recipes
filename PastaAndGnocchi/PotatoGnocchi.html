Making Potato Gnocchi
General Info
  • Old floury potatoes are best
  • Moisture in the potatoes is the enemy - baking them is probably best, although it's also common to boil them in their skins
  • Too much flour and gnocchi are gummy, too little and they fall apart. River cafe uses 100g flour and 1 egg to 1kg potatoes (raw weight), but most recipes say more like 300g and 1 egg yolk
  • Eggs help bind dough - not sure if this is good or not
  • Work fast, while the potatoes are hot
  • A ricer seems to be the best tool for breaking don potatoes. A grater or sieve are options too
  • Mix/Work the dough as little possible
  • A dough scraper can be used to chop the flour into the potato, rather than knead it too much
    • Method
  • Bake pototes in oven until done. Then break them open, and bake 10 minutes more
  • Scoop out the flesh, and rice immediately, all over a large work surface
  • Make a large jackon pollock by scattering over all the ingredients - salt (maybe 12g per kg potatoes), pepper, nutmeg, flour, beaten egg or yolk (optional), parmesan (optional)
  • Chop together with a scraper, and form a dough with as little kneading as possible
  • Gently work into a disc about 2cm thick, chop into 2cm slices, roll quickly, and chop into gnocchi
    • Potato Types
  • You're after the flouriest (mehlkochend) potatoes possible
  • Old soft dehydrated are better
  • Swiss mehlkochend varieties according to Kartoffel.ch - agria, belmonda, bintje, challenger, concordia, desiree, jelly, laura, marabel, victoria (Migros)
