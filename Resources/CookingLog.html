09/12/2020
Miso Soup
Hiroyuki Terada
Ingredients:
2 liters water
10 * 12cm kombu
40g bonito flakes
100g miso paste (caramel color) (optional)
20ml mirin
tofu
dried wakame

Method:
Heat cold water with kombu until boiling
Turn off heat, remove kombu, add bonito, let steep for 2 minutes then strain. Now you have dashi
Whisk the miso paste into the dashi and add the mirin
Serve with some tofu cubes and about half a teaspoon dried wakame per cup

Result:
Not bad, but could have been a bit stronger flavor. Maybe needed some extra kombu in the dashi, and letting the bonito flakes simmer in it for a while (not much though, as most recipes don't, or just very briefly)
Some suggest soaking the kombu in the water overnight before making the dashi
note: shouldn't let the water boil when the kombu is in the water. Take it out just before boiling (this is in all the recipes, and seems important)

Mandy Lee's Crispy Whole Fried Chicken
Mandy Lee on Youtube
Ingredients:
Chicken
Salt
butter
garlic
thyme

Method:
Flatten chicken - wing tips off, wishbone out, remove breast bone, cut around remaining bones. Basically get as flat as possible and o that all skin is on one side so it can be fried on that
Salt with 1% salt by weight, and leave in fridge
Put skin side down in a cold pan, and slowly fry over medium-low heat until skin is super golden crispy and chicken is nearly cooked through
Flip it, and add butter, garlic and thyme, and continue frying until cooked through

Result:
Very crispy skin, good flavor and moist

Potatoes in Stock and Cream
My Recipe
Ingredients:
5 Potatoes, Peeled & Sliced 6mm thick
Sprig of Thyme
40g Butter
1/4 Onion, finely sliced
1/2 Leek, sliced
300ml Stock
150ml Cream

Method:
Fry the onion and leek in half the butter until soft, then take off the heat
Meanwhile, fry the potatoes & thyme in the rest of the butter for 5-10 minutes
Add the stock to the potatoes, cover, and cook, stirring occasionally, until it is all absorbed
Top up with water as needed, and keep cooking with the cover on until the potatoes are cooked through
Add the cream, leek and onion to the potatoes
Check the seasoning, and finish in the oven for 15 minutes

Result:
Great!

Moro Orange Cake
Moro - The Cookbook
Ingredients:
4 eggs, separated
160g caster sugar
150g ground almonds
finely grated zest of 2 oranges
juice of 5 oranges, preferably Seville
juice of 1 lemon (if not using Seville oranges)
1 stick cinnamon
caster sugar to taste

Method:
Heat oven to 180C and Line Pan.
Put a tablespoon of the caster sugar in with the egg whites
Beat the rest of the caster sugar with the egg yolks until pale
Mix in the almonds and zest
Beat the egg whites and sugar until stiff and then fold into the egg mixture
Bake for about 60-70 minutes until golden on top and firm to the touch

To make some orange syrup, gently simmer the orange juice, lemon juice, cinnamon and a handful of sugar for about five minutes. Taste for sugar – the syrup should be quite tart. Allow to cool

When the cake is ready, remove it from the oven and allow to cool before opening the spring-form tin
Transfer it to a plate and pierce holes in the top
Soak about up to about half of the syrup into the torta

Serve the remaining syrup on the side

Result:
Technical problems, but taste was good.
Looks nothing like the one on the guardian page
Rose and sank pretty severely, leaving it looking like a skateboarding bowl. Maybe less egg white....or more?...internet suggests both. Seemed overly full in the first place.
Overcooked on top and bottom. Probably should reduce cooking temp a bit


Did various syrups with 50g sugar, 500ml orange juice, 40ml lemon juice (reduced by, 35%, 50%, 60%, 70%). 50% reduced might be good for penetrating the cake, and 60% reduced would be good for serving with the cake.
Few flaked almonds or pistachios on top would help appearance
If I can't stop this collapsing, one option would be to cut the top off and flip it. Possibly also candied orange slices in the bottom which would then be the top.

Beetroot Cured Salmon
300g salmon
1/2 small beetroot
Zest of 1 orange
Zest of 1 lemon
Large bunch of dill
32g kosher salt
30g sugar

Method:
Mix everything bar the dill and coat the salmon with the mixture.
Refrigerate for 24 hours, covered in plastic and weighted down with something.
Rinse the cure away, and let sit another half day in the fridge to allow salt to redistribute a bit.
Cover with finely chopped dill and serve.

Result:
Too salty, and not sure the beetroot brings much except color.
Should try again reducing either the salt or the cure time. (serious eats cure is 40g salt 25g sugar 900g salmon for 2 days)
White pepper is also a common addition. Coriander may also be good
