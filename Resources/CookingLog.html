Coffee Cake
My Recipe
Ingredients:
CAKE
125g walnuts
125g hazelnuts
150g butter
200g brown sugar
2 eggs
1 egg yolk
100g plain flour
1 tsp baking powder
3g salt
1tbsp instant coffee, dissolved in as little hot water as possible
30ml vanilla rum
100ml oil
ICING
160g icing sugar
80g butter
1 teaspoon instant coffee
1 tsp vanilla rum

Method:
Roast the nuts, then chop the walnuts, and grind the hazelnuts into powder
Cream the butter and sugar, stir in the nuts, and gradually beat in the eggs
Combine the flour, baking powder and salt, and fold into the mix
Combine the coffee vanilla and oil, and fold into the mix
Bake at 160C until done (around 40 minutes)

Result:
Really happy with this
The oil is a good addition as the cake is very moist, although i think i overdid it a bit. I reduced the oil and butter in the recipe a bit.
It uses less coffee in the cake than some of the other recipes I tried. This seems good - the other recipes used a huge amount of coffee, but even then the flavor was a bit lost. Relying on the coffee icing for coffee flavor seems to be better.

One Pot Chicken with Coconut Rice
Ingredients:

20g ginger
5 cloves garlic
1 tsp turmeric
0.75 tsp chili powder
2 chicken legs, without skin

1 sliced onion

Choux Pastry
French Cooking Academy
Ingredients:
200 ml of water
80 grams of butter
10 grams of Sugar
A pinch of Salt
3 eggs (for the dough)
1 egg (for the glazing)
100 grams of plain flour

Method:
Put the water, butter, sugar and salt in a saucepan, bring to the boil and simmer for a couple of minutes
Take off the heat, dump the flour in, and quickly whisk together until combined
Place back on a moderate heat, and cook while stirring until a crust forms on the bottom of the saucepam
Leave to cool a bit, the beat in the eggs one at a time (it will split and then come together as you add each egg). You should add the egg slowly toward the end until it reaches the correct consistency. The correct consistency occurs has been reached when you can hold a big spoonful sidewards, and it falls off slowly in a single large blob
Pipe into the desired shape
Knock off any tips by wetting your finger and poking them, before glazing with the final beaten egg
Cook at 200C fan for about 30 minutes, until well browned

Result:
Great.
There are a lot of variations in this
  • butter can be between 50% and 100% of the flour weight. More butter gives more crispness and flavor, although maybe more softness too (just my theory)
  • some use half milk, half water. More milk is said to give flavour and color, while more water should give a crisper result
  • sugar is not standard, but i guess it works for sweet recipes
See also

  • https://foodcrumbles.com/the-science-of-choux-pastry-in-profiteroles/
  • https://www.theflavorbender.com/how-to-make-perfect-choux-pastry

Burnt orange ice cream
Epicurious
Ingredients:
2 oranges (medium blond)
40g butter
300g sugar
500ml cream
150ml milk
2 egg yolks
2tsp vanilla
1.5g salt

Method:
Soften butter and rub over oranges
Dip oranges in sugar
Add 80g sugar to 25*20cm tray, and put oranges on top. Keep rest of sugar aside
Bake at 210 until sugar browning
Remove and reserve the sugar, then quarter the oranges and return them to oven until they get quite dark brown (chocolatey shade)
Scoop out the flesh of one orange and discard
Blend the sugar from the oven, the orange and orange peel, and the remaining sugar, then pass through a sieve
Heat the puree in a saucepan until bubbling, beat remaining ingredients together in a bowl, and then add the pure and whisk together
Chill and freeze

Result:
Not amazing, but interesting and pretty good. Bitter, but not too bitter - might go well with something very sweet

Miso Soup
Hiroyuki Terada
Ingredients:
2 liters water
10 * 12cm kombu
40g bonito flakes
100g miso paste (caramel color) (optional)
20ml mirin
tofu
dried wakame

Method:
Heat cold water with kombu until boiling
Turn off heat, remove kombu, add bonito, let steep for 2 minutes then strain. Now you have dashi
Whisk the miso paste into the dashi and add the mirin
Serve with some tofu cubes and about half a teaspoon dried wakame per cup

Result:

note: shouldn't let the water boil when the kombu is in the water. Take it out just before boiling (this is in all the recipes, and seems important)
